Key features:
. flowers in +- interrupted spikes or paired on axillary peduncles
. upper leaf pairs fused around stem or not
. corolla glandulary-hairy, hairy, or not
. corolla color

==pink honeysuckle:L. hispidula
color: pink, red purple
. <b>flowers in +- interrupted spikes</b> on axillary peduncles or at ends of branches
. <b>upper leaf pairs fused around stem</b>
. <b>corolla glandular-hairy or hairy</b> [may be short and sparse]
. stipules +- conspicuous, at least near inflorescence
. pink flowers
. common in bay area

==chaparral honeysuckle:L. interrupta
https://calphotos.berkeley.edu/cgi/img_query?enlarge=6121+1611+3983+0002
. <b>flowers in +- interrupted spikes</b> on axillary peduncles or at ends of branches
. <b>upper leaf pairs fused around stem</b>
. <b>corolla glabrous</b>
. stipules 0
. cream-yellow flowers
. scattered around bay area

==johnston's honeysuckle:L. subspicata var. denudata
species: southern honeysuckle
color: cream, white
. <b>flowers in +- interrupted spikes</b> on axillary peduncles or at ends of branches
. upper leaf pairs not fused around stem
. corolla often hairy
. pale yellow flowers
. scattered around bay area

==coast twinberry:L. involucrata var. ledebourii
color: salmon, yellow, red
species: twinberry honeysuckle
xx:ba
. <b>flowers paired on axillary peduncles</b>
. <b>corolla < 20 mm</b>
. plant 1.5--3.6 m, erect
. corolla yellow, with strong orange or red tinge
. common near the coast and in the east bay from Chabot north

==japanese honeysuckle:L. japonica
color: white, cream, yellow
. <b>flowers paired on axillary peduncles</b>
. <b>corolla > 25 mm</b>
. plant twining
. corolla white turning yellow, often tinged +- purple
. scattered at the periphery of the bay area

Edibility: All parts of honeysuckles are generally poisonous unless specifically reported safe for a particular species. ^https://www.eattheweeds.com/tag/lonicera-hispidula/
