dandelion-like flowers

Member of sunflower family (family Asteraceae)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Key to tribe Cichorieae

For the purposes of this page, “dandelion-like” is defined as a plant in the sunflower family (family Asteraceae) in which the head is liguliflorous. This means that it is composed of only a single kind of ray-like corolla in a 3-D arrangement. This page collects in one place the dandelion-like plants with yellow inflorescences for easier comparison.

Caution: there are more dandelion-like flowers that are not yet listed on this page.

Compare with yellow daisies.

Key features

  • stem forked or not, hollow or not
  • shape of basal leaves
  • size of cauline leaves, sharp toothed or not, clasping or not, clasping lobes curled or not
  • involucre swollen at base or not
  • phyllaries in 2 series or many
  • shape of outer phyllaries
  • color of inner phyllaries
  • corolla ± red abasially or not

hawksbeard
