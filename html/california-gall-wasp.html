Caution: There may be other wild species of this genus not yet included in this guide.

  • forms on branches of white oaks (section Quercus), usually valley oak (Quercus lobata)
  • a large tan gall that looks similar to the fruit of california buckeye (Aesculus californica)
    • oak gall is attached to the side of a branch, may be on a tree with leaves, may be lumpy, has no lines on which to split
    • buckeye fruit hangs from the tip of a drooping twig, is typically seen only on leafless trees, and may be partially split in half to show a shiny brown layer beneath
