genus Lessingia

Member of sunflower family (family Asteraceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

There are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

 spring lessingia
Lessingia tenuis
spring lessingia
Lessingia tenuis
  • 3–25 flowers
  • corolla yellow with purple-brown band inside
    • peripheral flowers may have pink or purplish corolla
  • glands on stem and phyllaries
    • cauline leaves generally glandless
  • phyllary tips ± purple

valley lessingia
Lessingia pectinata

  • 15–30 flowers
  • corolla yellow with purple-brown band inside
  • glands on cauline leaves
  • phyllary tips generally green

san francisco lessingia
Lessingia germanorum

  • 20–40 flowers
  • corolla yellow with purple-brown band inside
  • plant generally glandless (rarely with sparse glands on phyllaries)
    • all others have glands somewhere

valley vinegar weed
Lessingia glandulifera

  • 10–30 flowers
  • corolla entirely yellow
  • generally with sessile glands on leaf margins, phyllary tips

lavender or white corolla

slenderstem lessingia
Lessingia nemaclada

  • 3–10 flowers
  • corolla generally white (occasionally pale lavender) with darker tube
  • phyllaries generally hairless (rarely sparsely hairy)
  • cauline leaves with sunken glands (and occasionally sessile and/or stalked glands)

mount tamalpais lessingia
Lessingia micradenia

  • 3–10 flowers
  • corolla white to pale lavender with darker tube
  • phyllaries generally hairless (rarely sparsely hairy)
  • cauline leaves glandless (occasionally with sparse stalked glands)

crystal springs lessingia
Lessingia arachnoidea

  • 8–18 flowers
  • corolla pink to lavender with darker tube
  • phyllaries cobwebby tomentose
  • caulline leaves glandless
  • involucre 4–8 mm long

woollyhead lessingia
Lessingia hololeuca

  • 10–20 flowers
  • corolla lavender with darker tube
  • phyllaries cobwebby tomentose
  • caulline leaves glandless
  • involucre 8–13 mm long
