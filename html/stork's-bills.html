stork’s-bills

Erodium spp.
Member of flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Key to geranium family (family Geraniaceae)

There are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

narrower, uniformly colored petals

Key feature: bristles on sepal tips, sepal length

common stork’s-bill
Erodium cicutarium
  • stem ± glandular-hairy?
  • sepals 3–5 mm, tip with one or more bristles
  • petals ± = sepals
  • leaf compound; leaflets deeply dissected
  • pedicel is often a deep red purple, but not always
  • flowers in a cluster
 musk stork’s-bill
Erodium moschatum
musk stork’s-bill
Erodium moschatum
  • stem short hairy
  • sepals 6–9 mm, tip glabrous [with knob, but no bristles]
  • petals 10–15 mm [often shorter]
  • leaf compound; leaflets lobed to shallowly divided
  • pedicel is often a green purple, but is often short and hidden by the flower
  • flowers in an umbel

 soft stork’s-bill
Erodium malacoides
soft stork’s-bill
Erodium malacoides
  • leaf simple, crenate to shallowly lobed, blade generally < petiole
  • sepals 4–6 mm, tip with one or more bristles
  • petals ± = sepals
  • petals tinged toward red-purple rather than the above flowers’ pale purple
  • Monte Bello, Mt. Diablo

rounder, purple-veined petals

Key feature: stem glandular or not, precise sepal length

 mediterranean stork’s-bill
Erodium botrys
mediterranean stork’s-bill
Erodium botrys
  • petals are rounder and with more prominent veins than in common/musk
  • stem short-hairy
  • sepals 10–13 mm, tip with one or more bristles
  • petals ± > sepals (usually hiding the sepals)
  • leaf lobed to dissected

hairy-pitted stork’s-bill
Erodium brachycarpum
  • petals are rounder and with more prominent veins than in common/musk
  • stem glandular-hairy
  • sepals 7–10 mm, tip with one or more bristles
  • petals ± > sepals (usually hiding the sepals)
  • leaf lobed
