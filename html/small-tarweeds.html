Key to tarweeds and allies (tribe Madieae)

Assisted with commentary from Ryan O’Dell: “I look at the combination of stem color (Hemizonella is usually a light purple color), involucre shape (Madia exigua is usually a nice, uniform urn), and growth form (Hemizonella is usually short: < 10 cm and decumbent).”

small tarweed
Madia exigua

  • stem up to 30+ cm, unknown color
  • disk flower glabrous
  • anthers yellow to ± brown
  • ray flower fruit compressed side-to-side or ± 3-angled in cross section with curved beak
  • sand or clay soils in open, grassy, or disturbed sites

See also the key to tarweeds (genus Madia).

opposite-leaved tarweed
Hemizonella minima

  • stem < 20 cm (often < 10 cm), usually light purple in color
  • disk flower hairy
  • anthers yellow
  • ray flower fruit compressed front-to-back with straight beak
  • gravelly or rocky sites, generally open sites among meadows, scrub, or forest
