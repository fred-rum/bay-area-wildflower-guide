small tarweed
Madia exigua
  • stem up to 30+ cm, unknown color
  • disk flower glabrous
  • anthers yellow to ± brown
  • ray flower fruit compressed side-to-side or ± 3-angled in cross section with curved beak
  • sand or clay soils in open, grassy, or disturbed sites

See also the key to tarweeds (genus Madia).

opposite-leaved tarweed
Hemizonella minima

  • stem < 20 cm (often < 10 cm and decumbent), usually light purple in color
  • disk flower hairy
  • anthers yellow
  • ray flower fruit compressed front-to-back with straight beak
  • gravelly or rocky sites, generally open sites among meadows, scrub, or forest
