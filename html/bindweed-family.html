bindweed family

family Convolvulaceae

Member of dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Monterey County Wildflowers has photos of the various identifying features for distinguishing the various bindweeds and morning glories.

See also devil’s trumpets (genus Datura), which have a generally longer calyx which is ribbed or angled toward the base.

Convolvulus arvensis
field bindweed
Convolvulus arvensis
  • stigma lobes linear to narrowly spoon-shaped, distinctly separated, tips acute [example]
  • calyx ± 5 mm
  • corolla ≤ 40 mm
  • anthers are often lined with pale-purple
  • bracts > 1 mm below calyx, not hiding it
 false bindweeds
genus Calystegia
false bindweeds
genus Calystegia
  • stigma lobes oblong, tips obtuse [example]
  • calyx 7–25 mm
  • corolla 20–75 mm
  • anthers seem to always be white, at least for the common species
