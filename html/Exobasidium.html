genus Exobasidium

Key to gall fungi

Caution: There may be other wild species of this genus not yet included in this guide.

This genus parasitizes manzanitas (genus Arctostaphylos) and others in the heath family (family Ericaceae).

Ron Russo’s popular reference for California galls lists azalea gall (Exobasidium vaccinii) as forming galls on numerous plant hosts. However, discussions on iNaturalist have cast doubt on this interpretation. A timeline:

  • 1885 Casey (free) briefly describes Exobasidium arctostaphyli.
  • 1915 Burt (free) lumps galls on many hosts into azalea gall (Exobasidium vaccinii).
  • 1959 Savile (req. subscription) agrees with Burt, but reinforces the Exobasidium arctostaphyli species. (I don’t have access to this one, so my summary is based on iNaturalist comments.)
  • 1981 Nannfeldt (citation only) proposes that azalea gall (Exobasidium vaccinii) is found only on lingonbery (Vaccinium vitis-idaea).
  • more recent studies tend to agree, e.g. as described in the introduction to 2012 Kennedy (free).
  • 2022 Schwab: adds Exobasidium arctostaphyli to iNaturalist, and Asquith in particular starts adding this ID to many observations.
    • Schwab believes that although the original 1885 description includes infections of both leaves and young shoots, only leaf infections should be included in Exobasidium arctostaphyli.
    • Infections of whole shoots should instead be identified only as genus Exobasidium until properly researched.
