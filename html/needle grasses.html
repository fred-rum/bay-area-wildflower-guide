needle grasses

Nassella spp.
Member of grasses (family Poaceae)

Except for old historical records and extremely rare plants that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

All have 1 floret per spikelet.

Key features:

  • palea grooved or flat
  • lemma features
  • awn length
  • stem diameter

palea grooved lengthwise

bristly needle grass
Piptochaetium fuscum

  • blade 0.8–1.5 mm wide
  • awn 10–16 mm long

stipoid ricegrass
Piptochaetium stipoides

  • blade 0.2–0.4 mm wide
  • awn 15–25 mm long

palea flat

lemma generally bumpy throughout, tip with crown

andean tussockgrass
Nassella manicata

  • lemma crown generally sharply constricted proximally, distinct from rest of lemma

foothill needle grass
Nassella lepida

  • lemma crown gradually expanded proximally, no more than ± distinct from lemma
  • lemma crown hairs 0.3–0.6 mm
  • awn 12–55 mm, ± 0.1 mm diameter
  • stem 0.8–1.2 mm diameter

nodding needle grass
Nassella cernua

  • lemma crown gradually expanded proximally, no more than ± distinct from lemma
  • lemma crown hairs 0.8–1.1 mm
  • awn 38–110 mm, 0.2–0.4 mm diameter
  • stem 1.0–3.1 mm diameter
  • lemma body in age glabrous in distal ¾ except on veins
 purple needlegrass
Nassella pulchra
purple needlegrass
Nassella pulchra
  • lemma crown gradually expanded proximally, no more than ± distinct from lemma
  • lemma crown hairs 0.8–1.1 mm
  • awn 38–110 mm, 0.2–0.4 mm diameter
  • stem 1.0–3.1 mm diameter
  • lemma body in age hairy throughout

smilo grass
Oloptum miliaceum

  • lemma body smooth throughout

mexican feathergrass
Nassella tenuissima
