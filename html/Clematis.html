Clematis spp.

Member of buttercup family (family Ranunculaceae)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Except for extremely rare plants that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

All Clematis are ± woody vines. Flowers have sepals only, no petals.

 pipestem clematis
Clematis lasiantha
pipestem clematis
Clematis lasiantha
  • leaflets generally 3–5
  • inflorescence generally 1-flowered
  • sepals 10–21 mm, ≫ stamens, hairy both abaxially and adaxially
  • flower Jan–Jun
  • Ecology: Hillsides, chaparral, open woodland
  • common

See also creamcups (Platystemon californicus).

western virgin’s bower
Clematis ligusticifolia

  • leaflets 5–15 [but CalPhotos shows many with 3]
  • inflorescence several- to many-flowered
  • sepals 6–10 mm, ± = stamens
  • flower Jun–Sep
  • Ecology: Along streams, wet places
  • scattered

old man’s beard
Clematis vitalba

  • leaflets usually 5
  • flowers in a terminal axillary panicle, i.e. in stalked clusters from the upper leaf axils
  • not in Jepson; info from here
  • rare, mostly in and near Muir Woods
