woodland stars

genus Lithophragma

Member of saxifrage family (family Saxifragaceae)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

There are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

 hillside woodland star
Lithophragma heterophyllum
hillside woodland star
Lithophragma heterophyllum
  • hypanthium base truncate
  • petals with 3 lobes

bolander’s woodland star
Lithophragma bolanderi

  • hypanthium base obtusely tapered or hemispheric
  • petals entire or with > 3 lobes
  • very rare: east bay
 san francisco woodland-star
Lithophragma affine
san francisco woodland-star
Lithophragma affine
  • hypanthium tapered to pedicel [example]
  • basal leaves lobed ± ≤ 50% to base
  • most proximal cauline leaf 3-lobed
  • petals white

pink woodland star
Lithophragma parviflorum var. parviflorum

  • basal leaves lobed > ¾ to base to 3-parted
  • most proximal cauline leaf 3-parted
  • petals white or pink
  • very rare
