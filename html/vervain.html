vervain

genus Verbena

Member of verbena family (family Verbenaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Except for historical records that I'm ignoring, there are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

 western vervain
Verbena lasiostachys
western vervain
Verbena lasiostachys
  • flower bract 1–4.5 mm, generally ≤ calyx
  • stem generally ascending to erect
  • most cauline leaf blades deeply 1–2-lobed near base, coarsely toothed
  • leaf 4–10 cm, petiole < 2 cm
  • common

purpletop vervain
Verbena bonariensis

  • flower bract 1–4.5 mm, generally ≤ calyx
  • stem generally ascending to erect
  • most cauline leaf blades generally unlobed, coarsely serrate
  • leaf 7–15 cm, generally clasping
  • rare

bigbract verbena
Verbena bracteata

  • flower bract 4–8 mm, 2–4 mm > calyx [longer than the flower, even]
  • stem prostrate to decumbent
  • leaf 1–3 cm, base tapered to ± flat petiole
  • very rare

Toxicity:
1 – Skin contact with these plants can cause symptoms ranging from redness, itching, and rash to painful blisters like skin burns.
