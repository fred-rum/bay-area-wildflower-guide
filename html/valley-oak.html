  • leaf:
    • margins deeply lobed (sinuses > 50% to midrib), without spines
      • sinuses typically wider than valley oak (Quercus lobata).
    • adaxially often ± shiny, dark green
    • abaxially fine-tomentose, dull to pale green
  • acorn cap 10–30 mm deep, often as wide as deep, all scales tubercled
  • nut 30–50 mm, generally long-conic, distally acute to ± obtuse
  • common

Toxicity of acorn, oak (Quercus spp.):
1 – Skin contact with these plants can cause symptoms ranging from redness, itching, and rash to painful blisters like skin burns.
3 – Ingestion of these plants is expected to cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea and other symptoms that may cause illness but is not life-threatening.
