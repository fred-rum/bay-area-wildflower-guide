genus Angelica

Member of carrot family (family Apiaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

There are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

 henderson’s angelica
Angelica hendersonii
henderson’s angelica
Angelica hendersonii
  • basal leaves 2–3-ternate-pinnate
  • leaflets bright green adaxially, double-serrate to crenate
  • common along the pacific coast

california angelica
Angelica californica

  • basal leaves 1-ternate-pinnate
  • plant glabrous to sparsely hairy
  • leaflets sharply serrate
  • common east of the bay and in Marin County

woolly angelica
Angelica tomentosa

  • basal leaves 2–3-ternate-pinnate
  • plant glaucous everywhere
  • leaflets serrate to entire
  • less common, but can be found anywhere
