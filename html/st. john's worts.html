st. john’s worts

Hypericum spp.
Member of st. john’s wort family (family Hypericaceae)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Except for historical records that I'm ignoring, there are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:

  • herb, subshrub, or shrub
  • petal length
  • leaf shape

Flowers are sorted by petal length within each category.

herb

bog st. john’s-wort
Hypericum anagalloides

  • herb
  • petals 2–4 mm
    • all others are > 5 mm

scouler’s st. john’s wort
Hypericum scouleri

  • herb
  • leaf ovate to elliptic, not folded, tip obtuse
  • sterile axillary branches generally < 2 cm
  • petals 7–12 mm
 common st. john’s-wort
Hypericum perforatum
common st. john’s-wort
Hypericum perforatum
  • herb
  • leaf narrowly oblong, not folded, tip obtuse
  • sterile axillary branches generally 2–10 cm
  • petals 8–12 mm
  • the only local member is ssp. perforatum

goldwire
Hypericum concinnum

  • herb
  • leaf linear to lanceolate, generally folded, tip acute
  • petals 10–15 mm

 tutsan

tutsan
Hypericum androsaemum
  • shrub
  • many erect, branching stems
  • petals 6–10 mm, ≤ sepals
  • waif at Sanborn, Berkeley Hills

canary island st. john’s wort
Hypericum canariense

  • shrub
  • stem many-branched
  • flowers generally > 1 per branch
  • petals 12–17 mm

Hypericum grandifolium

  • shrub
  • large, leathery leaves
  • petals up to ~22 mm
  • not in Jepson’s eFlora

  • shrub
  • stem 1 or few-branched
  • flowers generally 1 per stem
  • petals 25–40 mm
