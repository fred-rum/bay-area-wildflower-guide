shortpod mustard

Hirschfeldia incana

Member of yellow mustards
mustard family (family Brassicaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
yellow in flowering plants

There are no other wild species of this genus anywhere.

  • fruit appressed to stem
    • fruit does not have a square cross section; flattened at least in the upper part?
    • fruit 0.7–1.5 cm long, 1–1.7 mm wide
    • terminal segment conical, 3–6 mm long, 1–2 seeded
  • basal leaves in a rosette, pinnately lobed
  • cauline leaves dentate to pinnately lobed, petioled or sessile, not clasping
  • most leaves are in the basal rosette; compare to black mustard (Rhamphospermum nigrum)
  • leaf feels “fuzzy” [basal or cauline?] [IF]
  • vegetation is dull gray-green; compare to black mustard (Rhamphospermum nigrum) [IF]
  • leaves have regular lobes with a terminal lobe not much wider than the side lobes
    • many more basal lobes than black mustard (Rhamphospermum nigrum)
  • open flowers often overtop flower buds
    • sepals and buds are hairy [IF]; compare to black mustard (Rhamphospermum nigrum)
  • very common
