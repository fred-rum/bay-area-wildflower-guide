shortpod mustard

Hirschfeldia incana

Member of mustards
mustard family (family Brassicaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
yellow in flowering plants

There are no other wild species of this genus anywhere.

  • basal leaves in a rosette
  • sepals spreading to reflexed [but the few existing photos indicate erect or ascending, rarely spreading, never reflexed]
  • stigma not lobed
  • cauline leaves dentate to pinnately lobed, petioled or sessile
  • valves have 3 veins each (or rarely more)
  • seeds ovoid
  • very common
  • fruit is valved proximally, indehiscent distally
    • fruit linear, glabrous
    • valves have 3–7 prominent veins each
    • distal segment is conical
    • distal segment has 1 or 2 seeds
  • leaf feels “fuzzy”; compare to cabbages and mustards (genus Brassica)
  • vegetation is dull gray-green; compare to black mustard (Rhamphospermum nigrum)
  • leaves have regular lobes with a terminal lobe not much wider than the side lobes
    • many more basal lobes than black mustard (Rhamphospermum nigrum)
  • most leaves are in the basal rosette; compare to black mustard (Rhamphospermum nigrum)
  • fruit does not have a square cross section; flattened at least in the upper part?
  • sepals and buds are hairy; compare to black mustard (Rhamphospermum nigrum)
  • no leaves clasp the stem
  • open flowers often overtop flower buds
