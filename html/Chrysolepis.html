genus Chrysolepis

Member of beach family (family Fagaceae)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

There are no other wild species of this genus anywhere.

Chinquapins also occur in other genuses, but these are the only chinquapins in California.

Both local species are parasitized by chinquapin flower gall wasp (Dryocosmus castanopsidis).

 golden chinquapin
Chrysolepis chrysophylla
golden chinquapin
Chrysolepis chrysophylla
  • leaf lanceolate to oblong, 5–15 cm, tip long-tapered
  • common

bush chinquapin
Chrysolepis sempervirens

  • leaf ± elliptic, 2–8 cm, tip generally obtuse to rounded
  • very rare [may be a misidentification in the bay area]
