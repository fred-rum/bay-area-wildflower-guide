baldhip rose

Rosa gymnocarpa

Member of roses (genus Rosa)
rose tribe (tribe Roseae)
rose family (family Rosaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
red purple, pink in flowering plants

  • loose shrub
  • prickles 2–8 mm, ± slender, straight
  • leaf axis generally glabrous
  • leaflets glabrous
  • sepals glandular or not, entire
  • petals ± 10 mm, pink to red
  • very common in the bay area

Toxicity:
except for thorns of rose (Rosa spp.):

0 – Non-toxic.
rose hips of rosehips, hip berries (Rosa spp.):
0 – Non-toxic.
