genus Trichostema

Member of mint family (family Lamiaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

There are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

 vinegar weed
Trichostema lanceolatum
vinegar weed
Trichostema lanceolatum
  • corolla tube 5–10 mm, curved upward and sharply recurved near throat
    • CalPhotos show that the corolla is abaxially sparsely hairy
  • stamens 13–20 mm
  • uppermost calyx lobe generally narrower
  • leaves lanceolate to narrowly ovate
  • leaves have a strong vinegar odor

woolly bluecurls
Trichostema lanatum

  • corolla tube 9–14 mm, straight
  • inflorescence hairs densely woolly, 2–3 mm, obscuring pedicel
    • CalPhotos show that the corolla is abaxially densely hairy
  • stamens 25–40 mm
  • calyx lobes ± equal
  • leaves linear
