genus Medicago

Member of small yellow peas
legume family (family Fabaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)

There are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

3–6 mm yellow flower

 bur clover
Medicago polymorpha
bur clover
Medicago polymorpha
  • generally glabrous
  • stem prostrate, mat-forming, or ascending
  • leaflet without dark central spot, length 1–2 × width
  • stipules generally cut ± ≥ ½ width
  • corolla 3.5–6 mm, yellow
  • very common
  • see also chile trefoil (Acmispon wrangelianus), which has generally 4 leaflets instead of 3
 spotted medick
Medicago arabica
spotted medick
Medicago arabica
  • hair 0 to sparse
  • stem generally sprawling
  • leaflets with dark central spot, length ± = width
  • stipules generally cut ¼–½ width
  • corolla 4–5 mm, yellow
  • uncommon

button medick
Medicago orbicularis

  • ± glabrous
  • stem spreading, mat-forming
  • stipules generally divided ± to base into a cluster of bristles
  • corolla 3–6 mm, yellow with ± dark purple spot on back of banner
  • Jepson says it’s a waif, but its been recorded around Quicksilver for 50 years now
 black medick
Medicago lupulina
black medick
Medicago lupulina
  • hairy or glandular-hairy
  • stem prostrate to ascending, angled
  • stipules entire or toothed
  • corolla 2–3 mm, yellow
  • uncommon
  • looks similar to lesser hop trefoil (Trifolium dubium), but black medick has the following characteristics:
    • leaf is hairy everywhere, has a distinct tip
    • elliptical tan fruit is hidden by drooping flower petals
