biscuitroots

genus Lomatium

Member of carrot family (family Apiaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Except for historical records that I'm ignoring, there are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

Caution: IDs uncertain. I don’t really have these figured out. More field observation may be useful. I should probably also look into fruit characteristics as necessary.

Key features:

  • leaf segments few and large or many and small
  • stem present beneath compound umbel structure or not
  • peduncle distally swollen/inflated or not

alkali desertparsley

  • plant stem ± 0, cauline leaves 0, occasionally 1 or more at base of short stem
  • leaf segments thread-like to linear, long, not crowded; herbage green, generally shiny
  • uncommon, scattered
