american asters

genus Symphyotrichum

Member of white daisies
sunflower family (family Asteraceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Except for old historical records and extremely rare examples that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

outer phyllaries pale-margined > ½ length, length generally ≥ 3 × width

 pacific aster
Symphyotrichum chilense
pacific aster
Symphyotrichum chilense
  • stem hairy distally
  • outer phyllaries pale-margined > ½ length, length generally ≥ 3 × width, obtuse
  • disk flowers: yellow
  • ray flowers: 8–12 mm, violet
  • very common, especially in the Santa Cruz range

douglas’ aster
Symphyotrichum subspicatum

  • stem ± hairy distally
  • outer phyllaries pale-margined > ½ length, length generally ≥ 3 × width, ± acute
  • disk flowers: yellow
  • ray flowers: 8–16 mm, violet
  • very rare

outer phyllaries pale-margined < ½ length, length generally < 3 × width

siskiyou aster
Symphyotrichum lanceolatum var. hesperium

  • outer phyllaries pale-margined < ½ length, length generally < 3 × width
  • stem ± hairy in lines
  • inflorescence bracts well developed
  • ray flowers: 6–12 mm, ± white to violet
  • rare

suisun marsh aster
Symphyotrichum lentum

  • outer phyllaries pale-margined < ½ length, length generally < 3 × width
  • stem ± glabrous
  • inflorescence bracts small
  • ray flowers: 8–14 mm, violet
  • rare

annual saltmarsh aster
Symphyotrichum subulatum

  • stem ± glabrous
  • disk flowers: yellow
  • ray flowers: 1.5–3 mm, ± white to pink
  • rare
