cypresses

genus Hesperocyparis
Member of conifers (class Pinopsida)

Except for old historical records and extremely rare examples that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

 monterey cypress
Hesperocyparis macrocarpa
monterey cypress
Hesperocyparis macrocarpa
  • crown asymmetric, often open, flat-topped to widely conic in age

sargent’s cypress
Hesperocyparis sargentii

  • crown symmetric, often dense, generally pyramidal in age
  • foliage dull, dusty to gray-green, glaucous or not
  • very rare

gowen cypress
Hesperocyparis goveniana

  • crown symmetric, often dense, generally pyramidal in age
  • foliage bright to dark green, not glaucous
  • plant 5–7 m
  • generally found on the Monterey Peninsula, much rarer in the Santa Cruz mountains

santa cruz cypress
Hesperocyparis abramsiana

  • crown symmetric, often dense, generally pyramidal in age
  • foliage bright to dark green, not glaucous
  • plant 6–10 m
  • found in the Santa Cruz mountains
