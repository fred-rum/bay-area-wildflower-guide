white mustard
Sinapis alba

  • basal leaves not in a rosette
  • sepals spreading
  • stigma 2-lobed
  • fruit is valved proximally, indehiscent distally
    • fruit linear, glabrous
    • valves have 3–7 prominent veins each
    • distal segment is at least as long as the proximal segment, flattened
    • seeds spheric
  • rare
  • very similar to charlock (Rhamphospermum arvense)

  • seeds ovoid
  • very common
