woodland madia
Anisocarpus madioides
  • rays 4–10 mm
  • anthers yellow [brush off the pollen to see]
  • phyllaries do not extend partway up petal (example)
  • distal leaves and peduncle bracts are not tipped with spines
  • seeds often have plumes
  • Perhaps the disk diameter is smaller relative to the ray petals when compared to a similar-sized common madia.

See also tarweeds (genus Madia) for more flowers called “madia”.
