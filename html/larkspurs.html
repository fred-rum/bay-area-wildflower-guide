larkspurs

Delphinium spp.
Member of buttercup family (family Ranunculaceae)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Except for old historical records and extremely rare plants that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

The parts of a larkspur flower are described in the larkspur glossary.

Key features:

  • number of flowers and whether the oldest (lowest) open-flowered pedicels are > 25 mm and > 2 × youngest
  • color of sepals, reflexed or not (especially lower sepals)
  • multiple stems or just one
  • stem length, hairy or ± glabrous and ridged or not (especially at base)
  • length of lower petal blades, and width of petal lobes
  • proximal petiole (leaf stem) hairs > 0.5 mm & straight, spreading, white, or [maybe] not
  • leaf hairy adaxially or not, abaxially or not

There may be additional useful features that I haven’t pulled from Jepson eFlora.

 red larkspur
Delphinium nudicaule
red larkspur
Delphinium nudicaule
  • sepals forward-pointing, scarlet to orange-red (dull yellow), lateral sepals 8–16 mm, spur 12–34 mm
  • petals ± glabrous to naked eye
  • stem 15–50 cm (–125), generally glabrous
  • common in bay area

See also california fuchsia (Epilobium canum), which has much longer filaments.

 zigzag larkspur
Delphinium patens
zigzag larkspur
Delphinium patens
  • number of flowers and pedicel length indeterminate
  • generally < 6 flowers on main axis? [how many axes are there?]; if more, oldest open-flowered pedicels generally > 25 mm and > 2 × youngest
  • sepals bright or dark blue [or purple, derp], reflexed, generally glabrous
  • leaf generally on lower 1/3 of stem, ± glabrous; basal, lower cauline divided > 80% to petiole; lobes 3–5, generally < 15 mm wide; terminal lobe widest near middle
  • lower petal blades 4–6 mm
  • stem 10–90 cm, ± glabrous
  • common in bay area
 coastal larkspur
Delphinium decorum
coastal larkspur
Delphinium decorum
  • 2–20 flowers
  • pedicels 10–63 mm, 10–25 mm apart
  • if ≥ 6 flowers, oldest open-flowered pedicels generally > 25 mm and > 2 × youngest
  • sepals dark blue-purple, puberulent abaxially, generally not reflexed
  • leaf generally basal, ± glabrous adaxially, generally ± puberulent abaxially and on margins; generally 5 lobes > 50% to petiole; lobes occasionally > 6 mm wide
  • lower petal blades 6-11 mm
  • stem 8–35 cm, ± erect, hairy
  • scattered around bay area
 california larkspur
Delphinium californicum
california larkspur
Delphinium californicum
  • generally > 50 flowers
  • generally ≥ 2 stems, 100–160 cm (60–220), generally puberulent
  • sepals forward-pointing, ± lavender or ± green-white depending on subspecies [maybe]
  • leaf lobes 3–15, 5–60 mm wide, tips ± sharply cut
  • common in bay area
 royal larkspur
Delphinium variegatum
royal larkspur
Delphinium variegatum
  • flowers on main axis generally < 10
  • pedicels ± ascending, 6–74 mm, 10–25 mm apart, generally puberulent
  • if ≤ 6 flowers on main axis, oldest open-flowered pedicel generally < 25 mm or < 2 × youngest
  • stem generally < 50 cm, base hairy
  • proximal petiole hairs > 0.5 mm, straight, spreading, white, and some not
  • flower spur 10–19 mm, straight or down-curved < 3 mm at tip
  • sepals generally dark royal-blue (white or lavendar)
  • lateral sepals 10–25 mm
  • leaf generally on proximal 1/3 of stem; lobes 3–15, generally overlapping
  • scattered around bay area

 eldorado larkspur

eldorado larkspur
Delphinium hansenii
  • flowers generally > 12
  • stem 25–180 cm; base puberulent to hairy
  • proximal petiole hairs > 0.5 mm, straight, spreading, white, and some not
  • sepals can be many possible colors
  • lateral sepals 7–13 mm
  • leaf hairy, especially abaxially; petioles hairy; lobes 3–18
  • Mt. Diablo
 foothill larkspur
Delphinium hesperium
foothill larkspur
Delphinium hesperium
  • flowers 5–100 [CalPhotos shows them all with similar pedicel lengths, not longer at the bottom]
  • stem 40–80 cm (10–120), base generally ridged
  • proximal petiole hairs are unknowable
  • blades of lower petals hairier adaxially (inner lobe) than abaxially (outer lobe)
  • lateral sepals 7–16 mm
  • sepals spreading, not reflexed, dark blue-purple or pale depending on subspecies
  • pedicels ascending at < 45° angle [from vertical? angle at tip?], puberulent
  • leaf adaxially ± glabrous, abaxially puberulent, prominently veined; lobes 3–14
  • common in bay area
 san bernardino larkspur
Delphinium parryi
san bernardino larkspur
Delphinium parryi

Caution: ID unsure.

  • flowers 3–60
  • pedicels ± ascending, 5–68 mm, 8–50 mm apart, generally puberulent
  • if ≤ 6 flowers, oldest open-flowered pedicel generally < 25 mm or < 2 × youngest
  • stem 15–80 cm (–110), base generally curly-puberulent, base not ridged
  • proximal petiole hairs are unknowable
  • blades of lower petals equally hairy adaxially, abaxially
  • leaf generally curly-puberulent; lobes 5–27, generally < 6 mm wide
  • Coe, near Cowell, and lots in Pinnacles
