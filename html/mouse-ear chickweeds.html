mouse-ear chickweeds

Cerastium spp.
Member of pink family (family Caryophyllaceae)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Except for historical records that I'm ignoring, there are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

Petals are generally lobed ≤ 50% of their length. If the petals are fully divided, see chickweeds (Stellaria spp.).

big chickweed
Cerastium fontanum ssp. vulgare

  • stems both non-flowering/mat-forming and flowering/±erect
  • petals 4–5 mm, 1.5 mm < to 0.5 mm > sepals
  • calyx 4.5–7 mm, hairy, ascending
  • scarious margin of most outer sepals 0.2–0.6 mm wide

field mouse-ear chickweed
Cerastium arvense ssp. strictum

  • stems both non-flowering/mat-forming and flowering/±erect
  • petals 7.5–9 mm, 3–6 mm > sepals
  • calyx 4.2–6 mm, glandular-hairy, rarely with hairs exceeding tip
  • scarious margin of outer sepals < 0.2 mm wide

sticky mouse-ear chickweed
Cerastium glomeratum

  • stems generally all flowering, ascending to erect
  • petals 3–5 mm, 1.5 mm < to 1 mm > sepals, often 0 on lateral branches
  • calyx 3.5–5 mm, glandular-hairy, with some hairs exceeding tip by 0.2–0.8 mm
  • scarious margin of most outer sepals < 0.1 mm wide
