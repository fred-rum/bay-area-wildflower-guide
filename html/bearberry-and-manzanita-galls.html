Tamalia coweni
  • forms on midribs or edges of leaves of many species of bearberries and manzanitas (genus Arctostaphylos), including bearberry (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi), but not big berry manzanita (Arctostaphylos glauca)
    • galls cause significant swelling and distortion of the leaf edge
    • compare to Exobasidium arctostaphyli, which causes only a minor blister

big berry manzanita leafgall aphid
Tamalia glaucensis
  • forms on midribs or edges of big berry manzanita (Arctostaphylos glauca)
    • galls cause significant swelling and distortion of the leaf edge
    • compare to Exobasidium arctostaphyli, which causes only a minor blister
  • more info in this paper [HTML].
 Exobasidium arctostaphyli
Exobasidium arctostaphyli
  • forms bright red blisters on the leaf surfaces of bearberries and manzanitas (genus Arctostaphylos), turning glaucous on the inner side
    • blisters cause only a minor buckling of the leaf surface
    • compare to ericaceous gall aphids (genus Tamalia), which cause signifant swelling and distortion of the leaf edge
  • see genus Exobasidium for discussion
 Exobasidium a-spp-whole-shoot-discoloration
Exobasidium a-spp-whole-shoot-discoloration
  • discolors entire shoots (branch and all leaves) of bearberries and manzanitas (genus Arctostaphylos)
  • see genus Exobasidium for discussion
