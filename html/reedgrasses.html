reedgrasses

genus Calamagrostis

Member of grasses (family Poaceae)
grasses, sedges, cattails, and allies (order Poales)
monocots (class Liliopsida)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Except for extremely rare examples that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

european marram grass
Calamagrostis arenaria

  • up to 1.2 meters high, with leaves up to 1 m long
  • inflorescence up to 30 cm long
  • leaf is curled into almost a circle along its length in dry conditions
  • grows on coastal sand dunes
  • highly invasive

pacific small-reedgrass
Calamagrostis nutkaensis

  • panicle ± open below, lower branches spreading to ascending, narrow above

serpentine reedgrass
Calamagrostis ophitidis

  • panicle ± dense, narrow, branches generally appressed
  • leaf blade with small white-opaque hooks/glands between the veins (visible at about 10x magnification)
  • generally on serpentine soils
 pine reed grass
Calamagrostis rubescens
pine reed grass
Calamagrostis rubescens
  • panicle dense to ± open
  • leaf blade without small white-opaque hooks/glands between the veins
  • awn 2–4 mm
  • leaf collar generally puberulent or hairy-tufted
  • rhizomes 10–20 cm long, 1.5–2 mm thick, slender

fire reedgrass
Calamagrostis koelerioides

  • panicle generally dense, narrow, branches appressed
  • leaf blade without small white-opaque hooks/glands between the veins
  • awn 4–5.5 mm
  • leaf collar scabrous or smooth
  • rhizomes 2–6 cm long, 2–4 mm thick, stout
  • early colonizer of burned areas
  • very rare
