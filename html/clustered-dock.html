clustered dock

Rumex conglomeratus

Key to docks (genus Rumex)

Member of knotweed family (family Polygonaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
red, other colors in flowering plants

  • inner perianth lobe margin generally entire (if toothed or notched, teeth or notches < 0.3 mm)
  • inflorescence ± open (between whorls)
  • pedicels 1–4(5) mm, generally ± ≥ inner perianth
  • inner perianth lobes 2–3 mm, 1–1.6(2) mm wide
  • tubercles 3, ± equal, almost as wide as lobe [illustration]
  • leaf margin ± flat
  • common

Toxicity:
2b – These plants also contain oxalate crystals but they do not cause immediate problems. These plants have tiny crystals that lodge in the kidneys and can cause kidney damage as well as nausea, vomiting and diarrhea.
