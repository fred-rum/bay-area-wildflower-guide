dogwoods

Cornus spp.
Member of dogwood family (family Cornaceae)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

There are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

 red osier dogwood
Cornus sericea
red osier dogwood
Cornus sericea
  • inflorescence open, generally appearing with or after leaves, not subtended by bracts
  • leaf blade generally 5–10 cm with 4–7 pairs of veins

brown dogwood
Cornus glabrata

  • inflorescence open, generally appearing with or after leaves, not subtended by bracts
  • leaf blade generally 2–5 cm with 3–4 pairs of veins

pacific dogwood
Cornus nuttallii

  • inflorescence head- or umbel-like, generally appearing before leaves, subtended by 4–7 showy bracts
  • leaf blade 6–12 cm
