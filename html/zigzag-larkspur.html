zigzag larkspur

Delphinium patens

Member of larkspurs (genus Delphinium)
buttercup family (family Ranunculaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
purple in flowering plants

This species has subspecies or variants that don't seem worth distinguishing.

  • number of flowers and pedicel length indeterminate
  • generally < 6 flowers on main axis? [how many axes are there?]; if more, oldest open-flowered pedicels generally > 25 mm and > 2 × youngest
  • sepals bright or dark blue [or purple, derp], reflexed, generally glabrous
  • leaf generally on lower 1/3 of stem, ± glabrous; basal, lower cauline divided > 80% to petiole; lobes 3–5, generally < 15 mm wide; terminal lobe widest near middle
  • lower petal blades 4–6 mm
  • stem 10–90 cm, ± glabrous
  • common in bay area

Toxicity of delphinium, larkspur, rocket larkspur (Delphinium spp.):
4 – Ingestion of these plants, especially in large amounts, is expected to cause serious effects to the heart, liver, kidneys or brain. If ingested in any amount, call the poison center immediately.
