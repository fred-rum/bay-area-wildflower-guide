spring beauties

genus Claytonia

Member of pussypaw family (family Montiaceae)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

There are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

All bay area species are in section Limnia.

Caution: IDs uncertain

Plants have a rosette of basal leaves which may be green to red, prostrate to erect. The leaf blades may be inconspicuous, making the basal leaves look thoroughly unleaflike. They may instead look very stem-like. Be sure to isolate and highlight the basal leaves for photography.

Plants have exactly 2 opposite cauline leaves per stem, often fused or partially fused into a disc.

Key features:

  • shape of basal leaves
  • shape of cordate leaves
  • number of bracts per inflorescence
  • flower petal length
 candy flower
Claytonia sibirica
candy flower
Claytonia sibirica
  • cauline leaves are substantial but not fused
  • bracts throughout inflorescence, 1 for every 1–2 flowers
    • all others have only a single bract, below the lowest flower
  • corolla pink
 gypsum springbeauty
Claytonia gypsophiloides
gypsum springbeauty
Claytonia gypsophiloides
  • cauline leaves are quite small and ± linear [info from CalPhotos; Jepson is much less descriptive]
  • petals 5–8 mm
  • corolla pink

serpentine springbeauty
Claytonia exigua ssp. exigua

  • cauline leaves free and opposite in shape of “crab claws”
  • petals 2–5 mm
  • corolla white or pink

glaucous springbeauty
Claytonia exigua ssp. glauca

  • cauline leaves fused into ± disk
  • petals ± 2 mm
  • corolla white or pink

redstem springbeauty
Claytonia rubra

  • cauline leaves substantial, ± fused
  • basal leaf 1–8 cm long including petiole, blade < 2 cm, < 3× longer than wide, base truncate to wedge-shaped
  • many basal leaves, prostrate to spreading, smaller inward
  • corolla white or pale pink

streambank springbeauty
Claytonia parviflora

  • cauline leaves substantial, fully fused (a common subspecies) or free, linear, and long (a rare subspecies)
  • basal leaf 1–18 cm long including petiole, blade > 3× longer than wide, base tapered gradually to petiole
  • corolla white or pink
 miner’s lettuce
Claytonia perfoliata
miner’s lettuce
Claytonia perfoliata
  • cauline leaves substantial, fully fused
  • basal leaf 1–25 cm long including petiole, blade < 4 cm, < 3× longer than wide, elliptic to reniform
  • few to many basal leaves, spreading to erect, ± equal
  • corolla white or pink
