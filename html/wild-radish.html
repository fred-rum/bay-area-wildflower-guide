wild radish

Raphanus sativus

Member of radishes (genus Raphanus)
mustard family (family Brassicaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
purple, pale purple in flowering plants

  • petals pink to purple (or rarely white; check for pale purple veins?)
  • basal leaf blade 2–60 cm long, 1–20 cm wide, entire or pinnately lobed or divided
    • 1–12 lateral lobes, dentate
  • fruit is 2–6 cm long, 5–10 mm wide
    • not constricted between the seeds and does not break up into one-seeded segments when mature

Toxicity (leaves):
1 – Skin contact with these plants can cause symptoms ranging from redness, itching, and rash to painful blisters like skin burns.
