hairy-pitted stork’s-bill

Erodium brachycarpum
Member of geranium family (family Geraniaceae)
purple in flowering plants

Key to stork’s-bills (genus Erodium)

  • petals are rounder and with more prominent veins than in common/musk
  • stem glandular-hairy [not the pedicel]
  • sepals 7–10 mm, tip with one or more bristles
  • petals ± > sepals (usually hiding the sepals)
  • corolla bilaterally symmetric, e.g. with two slightly larger petals above three slightly smaller ones
  • leaf lobed
  • blooms March-August
