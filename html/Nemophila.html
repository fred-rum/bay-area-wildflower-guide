baby blue eyes and allies

genus Nemophila

Member of borage family (family Boraginaceae)
borages (order Boraginales)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

There are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

corolla ≥ 10 mm wide

 baby blue eyes
Nemophila menziesii
baby blue eyes
Nemophila menziesii
  • corolla 6–40 mm wide [generally at least 15 mm wide]
  • bright blue to white, white center, blue-veined, black-dotted, not purple-spotted at lobe tips
  • common throughout the bay area

fivespot
Nemophila maculata

  • corolla 10–50 mm wide
  • white with dark veins, dots, purple-spotted at lobe tips
  • very rare in the bay area

corolla < 10 mm wide

eastwood’s nemophila
Nemophila pulchella

  • corolla rotate, 5–12 mm wide
  • white or blue
  • pedicel 10–30 mm (longer in fruit)
  • extremely rare in the bay area

meadow nemophila
Nemophila pedunculata

  • corolla bowl- or bell-shaped, 2–8 mm wide
  • white or blue, generally dark-veined or with dark dots, may be purple-spotted at lobe tips
  • less common in the bay area
 white nemophila
Nemophila heterophylla
white nemophila
Nemophila heterophylla
  • corolla bowl-shaped, 4–12 mm wide
  • white or ± blue, without dark veins or dots
  • pedicel < 10 mm [many photos imply longer, however] (longer in fruit)
  • style 2–4 mm
  • lower leaves deep-lobed with similar lobes, generally well separated, stalked
  • scattered throughout the bay area
 small-flowered nemophila
Nemophila parviflora
small-flowered nemophila
Nemophila parviflora
  • corolla bell-shaped, 1–5 mm wide
  • white or blue, without dark veins or dots
  • style < 2 mm
  • lower leaves cut or shallow-lobed with dissimilar lobes, merging or not stalked
  • scattered throughout the bay area
