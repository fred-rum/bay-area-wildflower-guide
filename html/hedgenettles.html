hedgenettles

genus Stachys

Member of mint family (family Lamiaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Except for extremely rare examples that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:

  • pouch at base of corolla or not
  • inflorescence elongated or compact
  • leaf hairs long & silky or not

See also the similar-looking lemon balm (Melissa officinalis).

 rough hedgenettle
Stachys rigida
rough hedgenettle
Stachys rigida
  • pouch at base of corolla [example]
  • inflorescence elongated
  • leaf blade 5–9 cm, ovate to lanceolate, base rounded to cordate, tip acute to obtuse; glabrous to soft-hairy (not silky), occasionally glandular
  • stem, herbage ± glandular, not resinous; lower corolla lip 4–6 mm wide
  • corolla pink, bracts rapidly reduced distally on stem

short-spiked hedge nettle
Stachys pycnantha
  • pouch at base of corolla
  • inflorescence compact
  • leaf blade 5–12 cm, ovate or lanceolate to oblong, crenate to serrate, base rounded to cordate, tip obtuse; soft- to stiff-hairy, glandular
  • corolla ± white to ± pink

coastal hedge-nettle
Stachys chamissonis
  • corolla tube 18–24 mm
    • all others are < 15 mm
  • leaf blade 6–18 cm, deltoid to ovate, soft-hairy, densely glandular abaxially
  • corolla deep magenta to purple
