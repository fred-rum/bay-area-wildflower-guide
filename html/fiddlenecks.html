fiddlenecks

Amsinckia spp.
Member of borage family (family Boraginaceae)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Except for extremely rare plants that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:

  • corolla diameter
  • corolla with 0 or 5 radial dark spots or 2 bilateral dark spots
  • corolla throat partially closed by 5 hairy appendages/wrinkles or not
  • 5 equal calyx lobes, or < 5 lobes unequal in width
  • leaf succulent and fine-toothed or not
 common fiddleneck
Amsinckia menziesii
common fiddleneck
Amsinckia menziesii
  • corolla radial, tube straight, limb 1–3 mm diameter, with 0 or 5 dark spots
  • stamens generally attached above middle of corolla tube, if below then style exserted
  • very common

 tarweed fiddleneck
Amsinckia lycopsoides
tarweed fiddleneck
Amsinckia lycopsoides
  • corolla radial, tube straight, limb 4–10 mm diameter, with 0 or 5 dark spots
  • corolla throat ± closed by 5 hairy appendages [really just wrinkles in the corolla limb?], tube constricted in lower 1/2
  • stamens attached below middle of corolla tube
  • style included
  • Mt. Diablo

large-flowered fiddleneck
Amsinckia grandiflora

  • corolla 14–20 mm, limb 10–15 mm diameter, red-orange
  • very rare: Mt. Diablo
 bent-flowered fiddleneck
Amsinckia lunaris
bent-flowered fiddleneck
Amsinckia lunaris
  • corolla bilateral, tube bent so that the limb is not perpendicular to the lower prt of the tube
  • corolla limb 4–7 mm diameter, generally with 2 dark spots
  • scattered, but with very few modern observations

 bristly fiddleneck
Amsinckia tessellata
bristly fiddleneck
Amsinckia tessellata
  • calyx lobes unequal in width, 2–4 from fusion below middle, notched at tip
  • corolla limb 2–10 mm diameter
  • Mt. Diablo, Sunol, Pinnacles
photo
 woolly breeches
Amsinckia spectabilis
woolly breeches
Amsinckia spectabilis
  • leaf ± succulent, fine-toothed
  • 5 calyx lobes ± equal in width not fused above base, or 2–3 unequal in width, ± 1/2 fused
  • corolla limb 5–14 mm diameter
  • scattered near the pacific coast
