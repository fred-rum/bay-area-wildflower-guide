melilots and sweetclovers

genus Melilotus

Member of legume family (family Fabaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

There are no other wild species of this genus in California.

 small melilot
Melilotus indicus
small melilot
Melilotus indicus
  • corolla 2.5–3 mm, yellow
  • inflorescence axis generally 1–2 cm when flowers open
  • stem 10–60 cm
  • corolla length is ± 1½–2½ × calyx length
  • wings length ± = banner length
  • wings and banner length ± ≥ keel length
  • leaflets 1–2.5 cm, oblanceolate to wedge-shaped-obovate, ± toothed
  • fruit 2–3 mm, bumpy or with faint lines
  • common

yellow sweetclover
Melilotus officinalis

  • corolla 4.5–7 mm, yellow
  • inflorescence axis generally 3–8 cm when flowers open
  • stem 50–200 cm
  • corolla length is ± 3–4 × calyx length
  • wings length ± = keel length
  • wings and keel length ≪ banner length
  • leaflets 1–2.5 cm, elliptic-oblong to obovate, ± toothed
  • fruit 3–5 mm, plump, irregularly cross-ridged
  • uncommon

white sweetclover
Melilotus albus

  • corolla 3.5–5 mm, white
  • inflorescence axis generally 3–8 cm when flowers open
  • stem 50–200 cm
  • leaflets 1–2.5 cm, elliptic-oblong to obovate, ± toothed
  • fruit 3–5 mm, with network of lines
  • uncommon
