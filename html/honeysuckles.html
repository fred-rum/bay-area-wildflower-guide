honeysuckles

genus Lonicera

Member of honeysuckle family (family Caprifoliaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Except for historical records that I'm ignoring, there are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:

  • flowers in ± interrupted spikes or paired on axillary peduncles
  • upper leaf pairs fused around stem or not
  • corolla glandulary-hairy, hairy, or not
  • corolla color

 coast twinberry
Lonicera involucrata var. ledebourii
coast twinberry
Lonicera involucrata var. ledebourii
  • flowers paired on axillary peduncles
  • corolla < 20 mm
  • plant 1.5–3.6 m, erect
  • corolla yellow, with strong orange or red tinge
  • common near the coast and in the east bay from Chabot north
 japanese honeysuckle
Lonicera japonica
japanese honeysuckle
Lonicera japonica
  • flowers paired on axillary peduncles
  • corolla > 25 mm
  • plant twining
  • corolla white turning yellow, often tinged ± purple
  • scattered at the periphery of the bay area

Edibility: All parts of honeysuckles are generally poisonous unless specifically reported safe for a particular species. [source]
