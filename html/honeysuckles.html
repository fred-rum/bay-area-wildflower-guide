honeysuckles

Lonicera spp.
Member of honeysuckle family (family Caprifoliaceae)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Except for historical records that I'm ignoring, there are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:

  • flowers in ± interrupted spikes or paired on axillary peduncles
  • upper leaf pairs fused around stem or not
  • corolla glandulary-hairy, hairy, or not
  • corolla color
 pink honeysuckle
Lonicera hispidula
pink honeysuckle
Lonicera hispidula
  • flowers in ± interrupted spikes on axillary peduncles or at ends of branches
  • upper leaf pairs fused around stem
  • corolla glandular-hairy or hairy [may be short and sparse]
  • stipules ± conspicuous, at least near inflorescence
  • pink flowers
  • common in bay area

chaparral honeysuckle
Lonicera interrupta
  • flowers in ± interrupted spikes on axillary peduncles or at ends of branches
  • upper leaf pairs fused around stem
  • corolla glabrous
  • stipules 0
  • cream-yellow flowers
  • scattered around bay area

 johnston’s honeysuckle
Lonicera subspicata var. denudata
johnston’s honeysuckle
Lonicera subspicata var. denudata
  • flowers in ± interrupted spikes on axillary peduncles or at ends of branches
  • upper leaf pairs not fused around stem
  • corolla often hairy
  • pale yellow flowers
  • scattered around bay area
 twinberry honeysuckle
Lonicera involucrata
twinberry honeysuckle
Lonicera involucrata
  • flowers paired on axillary peduncles
  • corolla < 20 mm
  • plant erect
  • corolla yellow, strongly tinged orange or red
  • common near the coast and in the east bay from Chabot north
 japanese honeysuckle
Lonicera japonica
japanese honeysuckle
Lonicera japonica
  • flowers paired on axillary peduncles
  • corolla > 25 mm
  • plant twining
  • corolla white turning yellow, often tinged ± purple
  • scattered at the periphery of the bay area
