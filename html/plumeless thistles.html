plumeless thistles

genus Carduus
Member of thistles
sunflower family (family Asteraceae)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

There are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:

  • number of heads per cluster
  • width of involucre
  • width and hairiness of phyllary bases
 italian thistle
Carduus pycnocephalus
italian thistle
Carduus pycnocephalus
  • generally 2–5 heads per cluster
  • involucre 1–2 cm diameter
  • phyllaries generally ≤ 2 mm wide
  • phyllary bases ± persistently loosely tomentose
  • very common throughout the bay area
photo
 slender thistle
Carduus tenuiflorus
slender thistle
Carduus tenuiflorus
  • 5–20 heads per cluster
  • involucre 1–2 cm diameter
  • phyllaries generally ≤ 2 mm wide
  • phyllary bases glabrous to sparsely tomentose
  • common throughout the bay area

musk thistle
Carduus nutans

  • heads generally solitary, conspicuously peduncled
  • involucre 2–7 cm diameter
  • phyllaries generally > 2 mm wide
  • La Honda Creek
