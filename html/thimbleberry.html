thimbleberry

Rubus parviflorus

Member of brambles (genus Rubus)
bramble tribe (tribe Rubeae)
rose family (family Rosaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
white in flowering plants

  • plant 0.5–2.5 m, erect
  • no prickles
  • stem: to 6 mm diam, not angled, glabrous to finely hairy with stalked glands, not glaucous
  • leaf: simple, palmately (3)5-lobed
  • stipules: triangular-lanceolate to narrow-ovate, 1.5–3 mm wide
    • all others are thread-like to linear, ≤ 1 mm wide
  • sepals hairy, stalked-glandular
  • fruit: raspberry-type (hollow when picked), red
  • parasitized by thimbleberry gallmaker (Diastrophus kincaidii)

Edibility: The fruit has a sweet and pleasant flavor, although it is very seedy. [source]
