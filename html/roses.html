roses

genus Rosa

Member of rose family (family Rosaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Except for extremely rare examples that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:

  • prickles slender or thick-based
  • size of shrub
  • sepals with lateral lobes or not, glands or not
  • length of petals

Parasitized by spiny leaf gall wasp (Diplolepis polita).

 ground rose
Rosa spithamea
ground rose
Rosa spithamea
  • dwarf shrub, generally < 50 cm
  • prickles 3–8(12) mm, generally slender (thick-based), ± straight [example]
  • leaf axis generally glabrous (finely hairy), glandular
  • leaflets 5–7(9), (±) glabrous
  • sepals generally glandular, entire
  • petals 10–15 mm, pink to red
  • common in the bay area

thick-based prickles

 california wild rose
Rosa californica
california wild rose
Rosa californica
  • shrub or thicket-forming, 80 cm – 2.5 m
  • prickles 3–15 mm, thick-based and compressed, generally curved [example]
  • leaf axis ± shaggy-hairy (± glabrous), hairs to 1 mm, glandless or glandular
  • leaflets 5–7(9), ± hairy, sometimes glandular
  • sepals glandular or not, entire
  • petals generally 15–25 mm, pink
  • very common in the bay area

 dog-rose
Rosa canina
dog-rose
Rosa canina
  • shrub or thicket-forming, generally 80 cm – 4 m
  • prickles ± few, 3–10 mm, thick-based and compressed, curved
  • leaf axis glabrous or with hairs to 1 mm, glandless
  • leaflets generally 5–7, (±) glabrous
  • sepals ± glandless, with toothed lateral lobes
  • rare
  • Nisene Marks, Marin, and Mt. Tam.
photo
 sweet-brier
Rosa rubiginosa
sweet-brier
Rosa rubiginosa
  • shrub or thicket-forming, 80 cm – 3 m
  • prickles ± 5–15 mm, thick-based and compressed, curved
  • leaf axis ± hairy, hairs 0.2–1 mm, glandular
  • leaflets generally 5–7, ± hairy, glandular
  • sepals glandular, with toothed lateral lobes
  • La Honda Creek OSP

iNaturalist may suggest woods’ rose (Rosa woodsii), but it’s not really in the bay area.
