Body parts

Image by Piotr Jaworski, PioM, CC BY-SA 3.0, via Wikimedia Commons.

head
The frontmost of the three major sections of the body, including the eyes, mouth, antennae, etc. [part A in the figure] [beetle glossary, true bug glossary]
thorax
The middle of the three major sections of the body. [part B in the figure] [beetle glossary, true bug glossary]
abdomen
The hindmost of the three major sections of the body. [part C in the figure] [beetle glossary, true bug glossary]
antenna
One of a pair of long sensory appendages on the front of the head. [part 1 in the figure] [true bug glossary]
prothorax
The anterior portion of the thorax, supporting the first pair of legs. [part 6 in the figure] [beetle glossary, true bug glossary]
pronotum
The dorsal sclerite covering the prothorax. [beetle glossary, true bug glossary]
forewing
One of the front pair of wings. [part 11 in the figure] [true bug glossary]
hindwing
One of the rear pair of wings. [part 12 in the figure] [true bug glossary]
sclerite
A plate forming one section of an exoskeleton.

Relative positions

anterior
Closer to the head end of the body.
posterior
Closer to the tail end of the body.
dorsal
Related to the back of the animal or closer to the back. Opposite ventral.
ventral
Related to the belly of the animal or closer to the belly. Opposite dorsal.
apical
Closer to the tip.
basal
Closer to the base.
