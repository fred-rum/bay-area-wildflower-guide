fringepods

genus Thysanocarpus

Member of mustard family (family Brassicaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

There are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:

  • shape and length of pedicels in fruit
  • shape of cauline leaves
  • color of fertile anthers in flower
 sand fringepod
Thysanocarpus curvipes
sand fringepod
Thysanocarpus curvipes
  • proximal pedicels in fruit recurved in a continuous arc, 3–7(12) mm
  • fruit 3–6(9) mm wide
  • cauline leaves lanceolate, bases lobed, clasping
  • basal leaves present
  • fertile anthers purple
  • common
 mountain fringepod
Thysanocarpus laciniatus
mountain fringepod
Thysanocarpus laciniatus
  • proximal pedicels in fruit recurved in a continuous arc, 2.5–6(8) mm
  • fruit 3–5 mm wide
  • cauline leaves linear to narrowly elliptic, bases wedge-shaped, ± lobed or not, ± not clasping
  • basal leaves present
  • fertile anthers purple
  • scattered

ribbed fringepod
Thysanocarpus radians

  • proximal pedicels in fruit ± straight except sharply reflexed near tips, 7–18 mm
  • fruit 7–10 mm wide
  • cauline leaves lanceolate to ovate-lanceolate, bases lobed, clasping
  • basal leaves present
  • fertile anthers yellow
  • rare
