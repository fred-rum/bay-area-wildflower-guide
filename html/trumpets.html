genus Collomia

Member of phlox family (family Polemoniaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Except for extremely rare examples that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

 variableleaf collomia
Collomia heterophylla
variableleaf collomia
Collomia heterophylla
  • corolla lobes red purple or pink to white, throat white, tube greenish yellow
    • corolla 10–14 mm long
  • stem branching and spreading with long glandular hairs
  • lower cauline leaves 1–2-pinnate lobed (upper leaves lobed or entire)
  • uncommon

serpentine collomia
Collomia diversifolia

  • corolla petals red purple to pink, throat white and yellow, tube red or brown
    • corolla 9–12 mm long
  • stem branching and spreading with long glandular hairs
  • lower cauline leaves ±3-toothed (upper leaves entire)
  • very rare, mostly on Mt. Diablo

grand collomia
Collomia grandiflora

  • corolla dusty orange, fading to white
    • corolla 15–30 mm long
  • stem erect and simple with short nonglandular hairs
  • rare
