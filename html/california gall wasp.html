california gall wasp

Andricus quercuscalifornicus

Key to gall wasps (family Cynipidae)

Caution: There may be other wild species of this genus not yet included in this guide.

  • parasitizes white oaks (Quercus spp.).
  • a large tan gall that looks similar to a the fruit of california buckeye (Aesculus californica).
    • oak gall is attached directly to a branch, may be on a tree with leaves, may be lumpy, has no lines on which to split
    • buckeye fruit hangs from the tip of a drooping stem, is typically seen only on leafless trees, and may be partially split in half to show a shiny brown layer beneath
