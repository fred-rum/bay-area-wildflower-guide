deathcamas

Toxicoscordion spp.
Member of trillium family (family Melanthiaceae)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

There are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

larger flowers

 fremont’s deathcamas
Toxicoscordion fremontii
fremont’s deathcamas
Toxicoscordion fremontii
  • petals 5–15 mm
  • stamens ± ½ perianth
  • leaf 20–50 cm (e.g. ½ length of stem), curved
  • inflorescence wide
  • common

smallflower deathcamas
Toxicoscordion fontanum

  • petals 4–15 mm
  • stamens ± = perianth
  • leaf erect, often > stem
  • inflorescence narrow, often pyramid-shaped in youth (example)
  • rare

smaller flowers

meadow deathcamas
Toxicoscordion venenosum

  • petals 4–6 mm
  • stamens usually = perianth, occasionally >
  • pedicels in fruit 3–25 mm, generally ascending (10–60° from axis)
  • plant glabrous
  • rare

smallflower deathcamas
Toxicoscordion micranthum

  • petals 3–8 mm
  • stamens ± = perianth
  • pedicels in fruit 12–40 mm, generally spreading (60–90° from axis), with tips upturned or not
  • plant scabrous below
  • extremely rare
