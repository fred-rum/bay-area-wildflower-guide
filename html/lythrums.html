lythrums

genus Lythrum
Member of lythrum family (family Lythraceae)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

There are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

1–2 flowers per axil

false grass-poly
Lythrum junceum
  • sprawling, many branches distal to the base
  • petals purple with white at the base
  • hypanthia purple-spotted
  • newly identified in the bay area (abstract) “Previous collections, dating to at least 1905, were misidentified as L. californicum, but differ in their sprawling habit, white-based petals, and purple-spotted hypanthia.”

hyssop loosestrife
Lythrum hyssopifolia
  • branches mostly at base, with no more than a few distal to the base
  • petals ± pale purple
  • hypanthium 4–5 × longer than wide
  • epicalyx lobes ± 2 × longer than sepals
  • common

three-bracted loosestrife
Lythrum tribracteatum

  • branches mostly at base, with no more than a few distal to the base
  • petals pale purple
  • hypanthium 8–10 × longer than wide
  • epicalyx lobes ± = sepals
  • very rare
