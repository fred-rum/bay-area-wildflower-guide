beardtongues

genus Penstemon
Member of plantain family (family Plantaginaceae)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Except for extremely rare examples that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

 bunchleaf penstemon
Penstemon heterophyllus
bunchleaf penstemon
Penstemon heterophyllus
  • anther sacs 2.2–3 mm, dehiscing at proximal end to 1/2–2/3 their length, inner margins long-hairy
    • for all others, anther sacs dehiscing full length, generally spreading widely apart; anthers glabrous
  • inflorescence ± glabrous
  • corolla 23–40 mm
  • staminode glabrous
  • common

scarlet bugler
Penstemon centranthifolius

  • inflorescence glabrous
  • flower red
    • all others purple-shaded
  • staminode glabrous
  • leaf thick, entire
  • uncommon

inflorescence (excluding corolla) glandular
staminode hairy

sierra beardtongue
Penstemon heterodoxus var. heterodoxus

  • inflorescence glandular
  • corolla 10–16 mm
  • staminode moderately yellow-hairy
  • leaf entire, sometimes folded lengthwise
  • extremely rare on Mt. Diablo

rattan’s beardtongue
Penstemon rattanii

  • inflorescence glandular
  • corolla 20–30 mm
  • staminode yellow-hairy
  • leaf thin, shallowly toothed
  • uncommon in the Santa Cruz range

grinnell’s beardtongue
Penstemon grinnellii var. scrophularioides

  • inflorescence glandular-hairy
  • corolla 26–35 mm
  • staminode exserted, densely yellow- or golden-hairy
  • leaf thick, generally folded lengthwise and arching, at least partially dentate
  • very rare
