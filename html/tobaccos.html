dicots (class Magnoliopsida)

Except for extremely rare examples that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

tree tobacco
Nicotiana glauca

  • shrub or small tree
    • all others are herbs
  • herbage glabrous, glaucous
    • others are generally glandular-hairy
 indian tobacco
Nicotiana quadrivalvis
indian tobacco
Nicotiana quadrivalvis
  • cauline leaves ± sessile (except lowest)
  • corolla limb 20–50 cm wide [diameter?]
  • calyx lobes ± unequal
  • not to be confused with the “indian tobacco” of the eastern US, Lobelia inflata, which is in a different family
 many-flowered tobacco
Nicotiana acuminata var. multiflora
many-flowered tobacco
Nicotiana acuminata var. multiflora
  • cauline leaves petioled
  • calyx dark-striped
    • calyx lobes generally ≥ tube, unequal
  • plant densely glandular hairy

coyote tobacco
Nicotiana attenuata

  • cauline leaves petioled
  • calyx not striped
    • calyx lobes < tube, ± equal
  • plant glabrous to ± sparsely glandular-hairy

Toxicity of nicotiana, tobacco (Nicotiana spp.):
4 – Ingestion of these plants, especially in large amounts, is expected to cause serious effects to the heart, liver, kidneys or brain. If ingested in any amount, call the poison center immediately.
