So far the only rose galls I have observed are ones that form on california wild rose (Rosa californica).

 teardrop gall wasp

teardrop gall wasp

 Periclistus pirata
Periclistus pirata
  • takes over the galls of nodular gall wasp (Diplolepis nodulosa) and thus indirectly forms within branches of california wild rose (Rosa californica)

nodular gall wasp
Diplolepis nodulosa

  • forms within branches of california wild rose (Rosa californica)
  • parasitized by Periclistus pirata
