Member of cactus family (family Cactaceae)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

There are no other species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features to verify O. ficus-indica:

  • “stem” velvety or minutely hairy or not, with bristles > 2 mm or inconspicuous
  • terminal stem segment length
  • fruit or flower color
  • fruit spiny or not, juicy or not
  • 1 erect trunk or several basal branches ± decumbent to ascending
  • overall height
  • number of areoles per ovary
    • ovary is the fist-sized protrusion that sprounts a flower
    • areoles are the spots where spines potentially sprout
