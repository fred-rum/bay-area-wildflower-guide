genus Strophiona

Key to longhorn beetles (family Cerambycidae)

There are no other wild species of this genus anywhere.

All 3 species of this genus can potentially be found in the bay area. Although chestnut bark borer (Strophiona nitens) isn’t supposed to be found in CA, iNaturalist has some local observations.

Key to the western members of longhorn beetles (family Cerambycidae). The western species of genus Strophiona in particular are distinguished on page 279, with key terms defined in the first few pages.

 Strophiona tigrina
Strophiona tigrina
  • anterior dark elytral band oblique [black space between frontmost yellow stripes at an angle]
  • center of pronotum [ahead of the wing cases] with at most sparse pubescence
  • most common

Strophiona laeta

  • anterior dark elytral band transverse [straight across]
  • pronotum with center obscured by dense [yellow] pubescence
  • rare

chestnut bark borer
Strophiona nitens

  • unknown markings
  • rare; not supposed to be in CA, but iNaturalist has some local observations
