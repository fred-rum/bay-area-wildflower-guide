mallows

genus Malva

Member of flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Key to mallow family (family Malvaceae)

There are no other wild species of this genus in California.

Mallows have an extra epicalyx cupping the calyx. (Jepson calls it bractlets.)

Peel back the calyx segments to view the fruit within.

There is a very good guide to the wild mallows of New Zealand that conveniently encompasses all Bay Area mallows.

epicalyx segments are fused to each other

 island mallow
Malva assurgentiflora
island mallow
Malva assurgentiflora
  • shrub
    • all others are an herb or subshrub
  • petals 25–45 mm
  • leaf has distinctive triangular lobes with triangular teeth

tree mallow
Malva arborea

  • petals 15–20 mm with 5 dark veins
    • petals are dark at the base
  • calyx ± 4 mm
  • epicalyx free parts ± 8 mm long, > calyx
 cretan mallow
Malva multiflora
cretan mallow
Malva multiflora
  • petals 10–16 mm, generally with 3 dark veins
    • petals are not dark at the base
  • calyx ± 4 mm
  • epicalyx < calyx

epicalyx segments are partially fused to calyx, separate from each other

 bull mallow
Malva nicaeensis
bull mallow
Malva nicaeensis
  • petals 5–12 mm, veins generally dark
  • sepals 4–6 mm
  • bractlets widely lanceolate to ovate, bases fused to calyx
  • fruit is reticulated

epicalyx segments are free

common mallow
Malva sylvestris

  • petals generally 15–30 mm, veins dark
    • all others below have shorter petals
  • sepals 6–8 mm
  • bractlets 3–7 mm long, 2–4 mm wide, free
  • fruit is reticulated
photo
 dwarf mallow
Malva neglecta
dwarf mallow
Malva neglecta
  • petals generally twice as long as sepals (8–12 mm)
  • sepals 4–6 mm
  • bractlets widely linear to narrowly lanceolate, bases free from each other and from calyx
  • fruit is smoothly ribbed

 cheeseweed mallow

cheeseweed mallow
Malva parviflora
  • petals 3–5 mm
  • sepals ~3 mm
  • bractlets 1–2 mm long, linear to thread-like
  • fruit is reticulated and always exposed by the calyx
