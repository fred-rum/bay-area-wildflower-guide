orchid glossary

Parts of an orchid flower

An orchid flower has three sepals and three petals in a bilateral arrangement (not radial). As usual, the petals are positioned inside the ring of sepals.

lower petal
The single petal in the lower (proximal) position on the flower. [figure] [larkspur glossary]
lip
The lower petal, often with a highly modified shape. [Jepson, flower glossary, blue-eyed marys]
upper petals
The two petals on either side of the flower and slightly above the midline. [figure] [larkspur glossary]
lateral petals
The upper petals.
upper sepal
The single sepal in the upper (distal) position on the flower. [figure] [larkspur glossary]
lower sepals
The two sepals on either side of the flower and slightly below the midline. [figure] [larkspur glossary]
lateral sepals
The lower sepals. [larkspur glossary]
column
A fusion of the stamens, style, and stigma. [Jepson]
