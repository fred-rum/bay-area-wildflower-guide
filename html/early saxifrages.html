early saxifrages

genus Micranthes
Member of saxifrage family (family Saxifragaceae)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Except for old historical records and extremely rare examples that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

 california saxifrage
Micranthes californica
california saxifrage
Micranthes californica
  • leaf blade shallowly toothed
  • inflorescence ± open, often 1-sided
  • flowers not clustered to loosely clustered
  • anthers purple to red (rarely orange)
  • petal length ± 2 × sepals

wholeleaf saxifrage
Micranthes integrifolia

  • leaf blade ± entire to minutely toothed
  • inflorescence never 1-sided
  • flowers loosely to densely clustered
  • anthers generally yellow to orange
  • petal lengths < 1.7 × sepals
