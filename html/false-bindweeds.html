false bindweeds

genus Calystegia

Member of flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Key to bindweed family (family Convolvulaceae)

Except for extremely rare examples that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:

  • small and narrow bracts up to an inch below the calyx, or snuggled up and wide and ± hiding the calyx
  • stem length, climbing or not
  • leaf shape, tomentose or not especially around sinus

Bracts > 1 mm below calyx, not hiding it, ≤ 5 mm wide or variously toothed or lobed like leaves

coast range false bindweed
Calystegia collina

It is unclear how to distinguish this from a hairy hillside false bindweed (Calystegia subacaulis ssp. subacaulis) except perhaps by stem length.

  • plant ± tomentose
  • stem decumbent, not or only weakly climbing, 8–15 cm, ≤ basal rosette of leaves
  • leaf reniform to distinctly lobed [tip may also be somewhat pointed]; margin generally wavy
  • Mt. Tam, Russian Ridge, Sierra Azul, Calero
