wood ferns

genus Dryopteris

Member of shieldfern family (family Dryopteridaceae)
eupolypods i (suborder Polypodiineae)
leptosporangiate ferns (subclass Polypodiidae)
ferns (class Polypodiopsida)

There are no other wild species of this genus in California.

 coastal woodfern
Dryopteris arguta
coastal woodfern
Dryopteris arguta
  • frond 1–2-pinnate
  • stipe generally > ⅓ frond length
  • longest pinnae generally near the base of the frond
  • veins extend into segment teeth

male fern
Dryopteris filix-mas

  • frond 1–2-pinnate
  • stipe generally ≤ ⅓ frond length
  • longest pinnae generally near the middle of the frond
  • veins generally don’t extend into segment teeth

spreading wood fern
Dryopteris expansa

  • frond ± 3-pinnate [final division is lobes, not teeth]
