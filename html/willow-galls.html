willow galls

Member of arthropods (phylum Arthropoda)
animals (kingdom Animalia)
galls

Gallformers.org lists particular species of willows (genus Salix) as hosts for each parasite, but it’s always a bunch of species. That makes me think that their lists might not be exhaustive, and in any case I’m not good enough at distinguishing willows to do anything with that info.

willow rosette gall midge
Rabdophaga salicisbrassicoides

  • forms a leafy bundle on or around a branch of willows (genus Salix)
 potato gall midge
Rabdophaga salicisbatatus
potato gall midge
Rabdophaga salicisbatatus
  • forms within branches of willows (genus Salix)
    • not necessarily at the end of a twig
    • reddish or greenish, bloated and lumpy
 willow beaked-gall midge
Rabdophaga rigidae
willow beaked-gall midge
Rabdophaga rigidae
  • forms within the tips of branches of willows (genus Salix)
    • at the end of a twig or at the base of a terminal leaf cluster
    • reddish or greenish, bloated and lumpy

willow cone gall midge
Rabdophaga strobiloides

  • forms at the end of twigs of willows (genus Salix)
    • gray-hairy over brown pinecone shape

willow rose gall midge
Rabdophaga rosaria

  • forms a green leafy bundle on tips of branches of willows (genus Salix)
 willow apple gall sawfly
Euura californica
willow apple gall sawfly
Euura californica
  • forms on leaf surfaces of willows (genus Salix)
    • smooth red blister

willow redgall sawfly
Euura proxima

  • forms on leaf surfaces of willows (genus Salix)
    • lumpy red blister(s)

arroyo willow stem sawfly
Euura lasiolepis

  • forms within branches of arroyo willow (Salix lasiolepis)

willow stem sawfly
Euura exiguae

  • forms within branches of willows (genus Salix)
 Iteomyia s-lasiolepis-tooth-gall
Iteomyia s-lasiolepis-tooth-gall

 Iteomyia s-lasiolepis-tube-gall
Iteomyia s-lasiolepis-tube-gall
  • forms within leaf surfaces of arroyo willow (Salix lasiolepis)
