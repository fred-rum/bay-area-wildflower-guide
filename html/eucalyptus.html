eucalyptus

genus Eucalyptus

Member of myrtle family (family Myrtaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Except for old historical records and extremely rare examples that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:

  • bark smooth, shedding, and light colored; or deeply furrowed and dark colored
  • leaf shape
  • inflorescence shape
  • number of flowers per umbel
  • flowers/fruit fused at base or free
  • hypanthium shape

All eucalypts are potentially parasitized by red gum lerp psyllid (Glycaspis brimblecombei).
