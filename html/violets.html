violets

genus Viola

Member of violet family (family Violaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Except for old historical records and extremely rare examples that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

Key features:

  • corolla color
  • leaves simple or compound
  • basal leaves or not; cauline leaves or not
  • stem ± erect, stolon-like [prostrate, root-forming], or not present
  • cleistogamous flower present or not

yellow violets

 california golden violet
Viola pedunculata
california golden violet
Viola pedunculata
  • leaves simple, cauline only
  • stems decumbent to erect, branched, with leaves, peduncles
  • no cleistogamous flower present
  • common
 goosefoot violet
Viola purpurea
goosefoot violet
Viola purpurea
  • leaves simple, basal and cauline
  • 1–6 basal leaves per caudex [subterranean woody base], petioles winged
  • stems prostrate to erect, with leaves, peduncles
  • cleistogamous flower present
  • scattered
 redwood violet
Viola sempervirens
redwood violet
Viola sempervirens
  • leaves simple; tip is usually rounded, not pointed
  • lower stem stolon-like [prostrate, root-forming], with evergreen leaves, peduncles
  • common in the Santa Cruz range and Oakland
 stream violet
Viola glabella
stream violet
Viola glabella
  • leaves simple, smaller, duller, and lower to the ground than redwood violet (Viola sempervirens); tip may be slightly pointed
  • lower stem erect, without leaves, peduncles
  • Santa Cruz range and Oakland

douglas’ violet
Viola douglasii

  • leaves 2-pinnate-compound
  • leaf blades longer than wide; leaf segments 3–5, 1–2.5(5) mm wide
  • lateral 2 petals bearded with cylindric hairs [near the throat]
  • Coe, Ohlone

shelton’s violet
Viola sheltonii

  • leaves ternate-compound
  • leaf blades wider than long; leaf segments 3, 2–10 mm wide
  • lateral 2 petals ± bearded or not with club-shaped hairs
  • Mt. Diablo, Ohlone

violet violets

 western dog violet
Viola adunca var. adunca
western dog violet
Viola adunca var. adunca
  • stems prostrate to erect, with leaves, peduncles
  • scattered near pacific coast and Oakland

eurasian sweet violet
Viola odorata

  • no stem [just a peduncle]
  • Bonny Doon, possibly Long Ridge
