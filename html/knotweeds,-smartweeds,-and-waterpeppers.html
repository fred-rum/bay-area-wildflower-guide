knotweeds, smartweeds, and waterpeppers

genus Persicaria

Member of knotweed family (family Polygonaceae)
dicots (class Magnoliopsida)
flowering plants (subphylum Angiospermae)

Except for extremely rare examples that I don't expect to encounter, there are no other wild species of this genus in the bay area.

See also knotweeds (genus Polygonum).

pink knotweed
Persicaria capitata

  • inflorescence ± head-like
    • all others are spike- or panicle-like
  • petioles generally winged with ear-like lobes at the base
    • in photos this looks more like narrow lobes that stick out or clasp the stem, and the petiole is too short to display separate wings
    • all below have no petioles or ear-like lobes

dotted smartweed

water smartweed
Persicaria amphibia

  • dense spike hides individual calyxes
  • flower pink to red purple
  • found in water

spotted lady’s thumb
Persicaria maculosa
  • dense spike hides individual calyxes
  • flower pink to red purple
  • no peduncle glands

pale smartweed
Persicaria lapathifolia

  • dense spike mostly hides individual calyxes
  • flower white to pink to red purple
  • ±stalked peduncle glands
